From Friction to Flow: How Strategic UX Changes Boosted Activation Rates

I redesigned the onboarding flow for a fintech app to improve KYC completion and wallet funding. Working closely with growth and engineering, we increased user activation from 8% to over 30%—the highest in two years.

For nearly two years, our team battled a stubborn bottleneck in user activation. While thousands signed up weekly, a significant portion stalled at critical hurdles:

  1. KYC verification (required to unlock core features).
  2. Initial wallet funding (the gateway to using the app's full functionality)
Despite marketing optimizations and backend improvements, our conversion rates remained frustratingly low. It wasn't just a metrics problem, it was actively blocking growth and preventing users from experiencing our product's value.

Why Users Were Stuck

Through behavioral data, we pinpointed two drop-off points:

  • KYC Roadblocks: Users abandoned verification when requirements felt unclear or overwhelming.
  • Wallet Funding Hesitation: Even verified users often didn't take the final step to add money, leaving the app unused.

Earlier efforts like a shelved KYC redesign focused on external fixes (partner integrations, "higher quality" leads). While helpful, they didn't address the core issue: our flows assumed too much and explained too little.

The Breakthrough: Clarity Over Simplicity

Our users, primarily in emerging markets, didn't want minimalist designs they wanted guidance. Key insights:

  • "Simple" UIs often lacked context, leaving users confused.
  • Progressive disclosure (showing only what's needed when it's needed) reduced overwhelm.
  • Visual cues and motivational copy significantly boosted action rates.

The Redesign: Small Changes, Big Impact

Pre-KYC Prep Screen

  • Added a checklist of required documents before starting verification.
  • Used icons and plain language to demystify the process.

Post KYC Momentum Builder

  • Replaced generic menus with a single, benefit driven CTA ("Add Money now").
  • Added social proof ("Join 1,000,000+") to build trust.

Flexible Funding Options

  • Created multiple entry points (e.g., "Fund to Get Your Virtual Card") to match diverse user goals.

Key Principle: Meet users where they are, anticipate doubts and resolve them before they cause drop offs.

Results: 30% KYC Completion, A 4x Lift

Within a month:

  • KYC completion surged from 8% to 30%—the highest in two years.
  • User feedback highlighted the "intuitive" and "reassuring" experience.
  • Activation rates rose, proving that design could unblock growth.

Lessons Learned

  • Familiar ≠ Effective: Jakob's Law matters, but our users needed more hand holding than industry norms.
  • Delays Can Be Strategic: The postponed redesign allowed deeper research, leading to a tailored solution.
  • Momentum Is Everything: Each screen was engineered to reduce friction and propel users forward.

Next Steps: Building on Momentum

With KYC and Add money success unlocked, i focused on deepening engagement and removing remaining friction points:

  • Guided First Transaction: Designing clear prompts to help users complete their first meaningful action (e.g., sending money or getting a virtual card) to reinforce the app's value immediately.
  • Streamlined Wallet Funding: Exploring faster funding options (like one-tap top-ups) to reduce barriers for users who completed KYC but hesitated at this final step.

Lesson: Solving one bottleneck reveals the next opportunity, great UX is an ongoing process of refinement.

Final Takeaway:

Great UX isn't just about aesthetics, it's about understanding behavior and designing for momentum. By replacing assumptions with empathy, we turned a stagnant funnel into a growth engine.
